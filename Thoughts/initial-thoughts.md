So this is a doc to put initial thoughts around the idea for the product that we are building. 

So the problem statement 
For users who are insured in India, one of the problems they face is having a fear whether their family members, friends, or whosoever needed are aware of their insurance policies, specifically when it is needed.

For example, let's say in case of a sudden admission to hospital, you yourself may not be in a position to pull out your insurance policy. The challenge is, does your family have your policy handy?

Second, say in case of death, unfortunately, do your dependents, be it wife or kid or even parents, have your policy handy which they can use to claim the claims? Third problem is, is there a place where all the policies are also kept handy, like we have Dizzy Locker in India, which records quite some ID proofs, or let's say we have the git repository for all code of the company, or say Google Drive to manage all necessary files? There is nothing which is there for insurance policies to do. Fourth, even if I have it, I may not have it very, very handy. I mean, it might be there in my email or in the respective login of respective insurers, etc. 


The solution thought: build an interface where users can do the following:
1. They can log in and identify where all policies are possible.
2. They can upload their policies manually under respective folders.
3. They can provide access to their email, which our system can read and extract only the insurance policies and feed it into the system.
4. There should be an option to share the policies with specific contacts, say wife or kid or parent, or even a very very close friend.
5. When that user logs in on this app, they should be able to check the policies shared by their friends and families, etc.

Key considerations 
1. Initially, we want to keep it very, very simple. It's almost like a repository where one can upload, manage all the policies, as well as share with necessary friends.
2. Family members and friends can access the shared policy when they log in to the app, and we should be able to link the phone numbers
Third, there is no monetization angle right now, but it can be planned in the second phase.


Think of this The only cost should be the tech cost of recording data and documentation, and that can be passed on as a cost to the customer later on in the future, once there is enough traction. 
In later phases, we can imagine that we can have functionalities where the key pointers of policies can be extracted by different AI models specific to insurance. We can inform users around what is available, what is included, and what is not included. Probably there can be recommendations around what inclusions they should look for in the future. 
 product being built by just two people, one tech person, one non-tech person, in basically an extremely lean fashion. 

Once all these traction are there, we can think of putting a small marketplace where insurers can talk about their policies and our users can access what is available and what is not available. Of course, if that has to come with necessary licenses requirement, we can provision those licenses. 

Another direction this can take is just the financial literacy part, where you have:
- Insurance wallet
- Credit reports
- All your statements (bank statement, etc.)
They can be necessary intelligence, recommendation, etc., that can be provided to the user.


The other direction this can take is the ability to create a family wallet for all non-monetary aspects, but more of repositories of important documents, proof, letters, etc. 

Almost like a Dizzy Locker for important things which may not be very retainable or shareable on Google Drive, WhatsApp, etc.
