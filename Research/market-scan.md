# Market Scan — Insurance Wallet / Policy Sharing Products in India

**Date:** March 2026
**Scope:** Third-party apps/products for storing, managing, and sharing insurance policies in India. Individual insurers' own apps are excluded.

---

## Direct / Close Competitors

### PolicyVault — Keewee Fintech Pvt Ltd (Lucknow)
- **Positioning:** Explicitly a "Smart Family Insurance Wallet"
- **Family/sharing feature:** YES — core positioning, but feature depth is unclear
- **Status:** Live on Google Play. Early-stage startup, Startup India recognized (DIPP153964)
- **Assessment:** The closest conceptual competitor. Same idea, but very limited traction and sparse public documentation. One to watch closely.

### OneInsure — Robinhood Insurance Broker Ltd (Mumbai)
- **Positioning:** "India's first insurance management app" — manage all policies from any insurer in one place
- **Family/sharing feature:** PARTIAL — "Nominee Support" lets you designate someone to execute your contingency plan. Framed as post-event access, not real-time family sharing.
- **Status:** Active and well-established. Available on iOS and Android. Partnerships with major insurers.
- **Assessment:** Strongest existing player in the "all policies in one place" space. Heavy services orientation (claims help, endorsements, renewals). More complex than MyInsuranceWallet's scope — and the family feature is contingency-oriented, not collaborative.

### Bima Central — CAMSRep / CAMS (Mumbai)
- **Positioning:** Insurance portfolio management platform built on IRDAI's regulated eInsurance Account (eIA) infrastructure
- **Family/sharing feature:** PARTIAL — Nominee and beneficiary management built in. "Proactive claim readiness for family members." But access appears to be post-death oriented, not a live shared wallet.
- **Status:** Active. Launched June 2024. 50,000+ downloads. Web app + iOS + Android.
- **Assessment:** Strong institutional backing (CAMS). Key advantage: policies auto-populate from insurers — no manual upload needed. Gap: family features are nominee management, not real-time sharing during the policyholder's lifetime.

### CoverShare
- **Positioning:** Policy sharing with trusted contacts
- **Family/sharing feature:** YES — sharing is core to the product
- **Status:** Known to be operating. Limited public information available.
- **Assessment:** Direct competitor in the sharing use case. Insufficient public data to assess feature depth or traction.

## Regulatory Infrastructure (Not Consumer Products)

### NSDL National Insurance Repository — NIR (NDML)
- **What it is:** IRDAI-licensed repository for electronic insurance policies (eIA accounts). Covers Life, Health, and Motor.
- **Family/sharing:** LIMITED — "Authorized Representative" access, but only triggered on policyholder's death or disability. Not a live sharing model.
- **Assessment:** Government-grade infrastructure. No consumer-friendly sharing UX. Not a direct competitor but relevant as background infrastructure.

### Centrico Insurance Repository — CIRL
- **What it is:** Another IRDAI-licensed Insurance Repository (formerly Central Insurance Repository Limited).
- **Family/sharing:** Same Authorized Representative model as NIR — death/disability triggered only.
- **Assessment:** Same as NIR. Regulatory infrastructure, not a consumer product.

### Bima Sugam — IRDAI / Bima Sugam India Federation (BSIF)
- **What it is:** Government-backed "UPI for Insurance" — a unified marketplace for buying, selling, renewing, and managing all policies. Rs 500 crore capital. Launching in phases through 2026: Motor (July), Health (August), Life (September).
- **Family/sharing:** Unknown — unlikely to be a Day 1 priority given its marketplace/infrastructure nature.
- **Assessment:** Not a direct competitor today. But as it becomes the default insurance infrastructure for India, it is worth monitoring. Could be an ecosystem partner or a long-term threat depending on how features evolve.

## The Gap — Where MyInsuranceWallet Sits

The market has three distinct layers, and none of them solve the real-time family sharing problem well:

1. **Institutional infrastructure** (NIR, CIRL, Bima Central, Bima Sugam) — powerful auto-population of policies from insurers, but family access is nominee/death-triggered, not a live shared wallet. Built for compliance, not for a stressed family member in a hospital corridor.

2. **Service platforms** (OneInsure) — capable and well-established, but complex and services-heavy. Family features are contingency-framed, not collaborative.

3. **Early-stage direct competitors** (PolicyVault, CoverShare) — closest in intent, but limited traction and unclear execution.

**The whitespace:** A polished, consumer-friendly product where a family can jointly access each other's insurance policies in real time — before anything bad happens — does not exist in a mature, well-executed form. That is the gap MyInsuranceWallet is targeting.
